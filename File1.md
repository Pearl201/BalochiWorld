

### **1. Baluchi Grammar Rules (Simplified)**

| Rule Category | Explanation | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Sentence Structure** | **Subject - Object - Verb (SOV)** | **Man** (I) yak suroup (an apple) **waan'** (eat). |
| **Formality** | Use `tao` for informal "you" and `shoma` for formal/plural "you". | `Tao chone?` (How are you? - informal) <br> `Shoma chone?` (How are you? - formal) |
| **Noun Cases** | Use suffixes to show location/direction. <br> **`-a`** = to/at <br> **`-saraa`** = by/with | `loug` (home) -> `loug-a` (to home) <br> `bus` (bus) -> `bus-e-saraa` (by bus) |
| **"To Be" Verb** | Often hidden or a short suffix. <br> **`-en'`** = is <br> **`-on'`/`-an'`** = am <br> **`-e`/`-en'`** = are | `Yeh kitab en'.` (This is a book.) <br> `Man washon'.` (I am fine.) <br> `Tao washe?` (Are you okay?) |
| **Negation** | Add **`Na-`** before the verb. | `Man na-kayaan'.` (I will not come.) <br> `Aa na-waarta.` (He did not eat.) |
| **Plurals** | Often formed with **`-an'`** or **`-aan`**. | `warnah` (young man) -> `warnahaan'` (young men) |
| **Adjectives** | Typically come before the noun they describe. | `zindag mardum` (alive person) <br> `kor morg` (blind chicken) |

### **2. Baluchi Tenses (Using `warag` - to eat)**

| Tense | English | Baluchi (I...) | Key Rule |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Present** | I eat | Man **waan'** | Stem + `-an'` / `-ii` / `-a` |
| **Past** | I ate | Man **waarton'** | Stem change + `-ta` / `-a` |
| **Future** | I will eat | Man **waaraan'** | Stem + `-aan'` |
| **Present Perfect** | I have eaten | Man **waarta** | Past Participle (`-ta`) |
| **Past Perfect** | I had eaten | Man **waartaa butta** | Past Participle + `butta` (was) |
| **Present Continuous**| I am eating | Man **waragaen'** | Stem + `-agaen'` |
